Help Khadijah and Her Family in Gaza

Khadijah dreamed of becoming a translator. She studied English literature with passion, imagining a future where she could bridge worlds through language.

All the mysteries of Agatha Christie’s that before had captivated her, now she finds herself in a mystery of her own.

A world that had been before so welcoming was now riddled with bullets and survival.

“My mum doesn’t let my dad go”

The only aid they get results in public shootings, so they resort to begging for help.

“It’s pride or survival”

Once, she was the cherished only daughter in her family… pampered and loved. But times have changed.

Today, Khadijah sells her gold jewelry to provide food for her younger brothers. Her family’s survival depends on her courage and sacrifice.

In a desperate act of hope, Khadijah walked 10 kilometers under the scorching sun, surrounded by a bunch of men, parched and exhausted. Driven by an unquenchable thirst and a heart full of resolve, she willingly handed herself over to the Israeli authorities — all for the chance to reunite with her family.

In the midst of it all Khadijah still transliterates poems, poems that shatter deep into your soul.

“Should I kill myself or have a cup of coffee?”

“In dreams you will lose your heartache”

It’s raw, unfiltered, and the most human thing I’ve ever felt ever.

“No one helps,” she said. The world turns a blind eye to her struggle.

But will you?
